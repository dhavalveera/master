Mysuru Palace

History of the Palace

  1. A Treatment to the irrespressible spirit of the people of Mysuru and their Kings, the Mysuru Palace has survived political upheavals, diaster and destruction, only to rise out of the ashes more magnificent than ever.
  2. The Current Mysuru Palace - the fourth to occupy this site - was designed by the British architech Henry Irwin after its predecessor was destroyed is a fire in 1897.
  3. The imposing building that stands today was completed in 1912, but it is believed that a Mysuru Palace was Established as part of a wooden fortrees, by the royal family of Mysuru, the Wodeyars, as early as the fourteenth century.
  4. In 1638 the palace was struck by lightning and rebuilt by Kantirava Narasa Raja Wodeyar (1638 - 1659 AD), who extended the existing structures, adding new pavilions.
  5. The glory of the new building was to prove short-lived. The death of Chikka Devaraja Wodeyar (1673 - 1704 AD) in the eighteenth century plunged the kingdom into a period of political instability.
  6. During these turbulent times the Mysuru Palace slipped into a state of neglect culminating in its demolition in 1793 by Tipu Sultan, the son of Hyder Ali, a maverick general in the king's army who rose to become the ruler of Mysuru.
  7. In 1799, when upon the death of Tipu Sultan the five-year old Krishnaraja Wodeyar III (1794 - 1868)AD assumed the throne, the coronation ceremony took place under a marquee. One of the King's first task, on his accession, was to commission a new palace built in the Hindu arcitectural style and completed in 1803.
  8. The hastily constructed palace soon fell into disrepair and in 1897 was razed the ground by a fire at the wedding ceremony of Princess Jayalakshmmanni.
  9. The destiny of the Mysuru Palace now passed to Queen Regent Kempananjammanni Vanivilasa Sanndhihana, who commissioned well-known British architech Henry Irwin to build a new palace that would be a tribute to the legacy of Mysuru & the Wodeyars.
  10. Completed in 1912 and at a cost of Rs. 41,47,913 the result was the Mysuru Palace you see standing today. A masterpiece in Indo-Saracenic architecture, on par with great Mughal residence of the North & the stately colonial public buildings of the South.

Meenakshi Temple

History of Temple

  1. the Temple is the geographic and ritual center of the ancient city of Madhurai and one of the largest temple complexes in Tamil Nadu.
  2. the Temple complex is divided into a number of concentric quadrangular enclosures contained by high masonry walls.
  3. It is one of the few temples in Tamil Nadu to have four entrances facing four direction.
  4. The corridor surrounding the sanctum the Meenakshi is called kilikoondu Mandapam ('bird cage corridor'). The space was once used to keep green parrots that were trained to utter the name of Meenakshi. There are two large cages full of squawking green parrots.
  5. The Kambatadi Mandapam ("Hall of temple tree") with its seated Nandi (sacred bull) has various manifestations of Shiva carved and also contains the famous "Marriage of Meenakshi" sculpture. Sculptures of Shiva and Kali trying to out-dance one another are pelted with balls of ghee by devotees. A golden flagstaff with 32 sections symbolizes the human backbone and is surrounded by various gods, including Durga and Siddar.
  6. The Puthu Mandapam ("new hall") constructed by Tirumala Nayak contains large number of sculptures. It is situated opposite to the east gopuram.
  7. The Ashta Shakthi Mandapam ("Hall of eight goddess") is the first hall in the entrance of Meenakshi shrine tower near to East Tower. Ashta indicates eight and Shakthi refers to goddess - the hall has statues of eight goddesses.
  8. The gopurams (towers) can be viewed from this hall. The passage was named for eight forms of goddess Sakthi carved on its pillars. Other sculptures and paintings depict the Tiruvilayadal (holy games of Shiva). The sculptures of heroes of Mahabharata, the Pancha pandavas can be seen in the Pancha Pandava Mandapam (Hall of Pandavas).
  9. The Viravasantharaya Mandapam is a large hall with huge corridors. To the south of this hall is the kalyana mandapam, to the south of the pillared hall, is where the marriage of Shiva and Parvati is celebrated every year during the Chithirai Festival in mid-April.
  10. The golden images of Meenakshi and Sundareswarar are carried into the 16th century oonjal mandapam (swing corridor) and placed on the swing every Friday at 5:30 p.m. The shrine has a 3-storied gopuram guarded by two stern dwarapalakas (guardians) and supported by golden, rectangular columns that bear lotus markings. Along the perimeter of the chamber, granite panels of the divine couple are present. The hall is situated in the western bank of the temple tank.
  11. The Mudali Pillai Mandapam or Iruttu Mandapam (Dark hall) is a wide and long hall built by Muthu Pillai during 1613. On the pillars of the halls, there are fine sculptures depicting the story of Shiva taking the form of Bikshadanar to teach the sages a lesson.
  12. The Mangayarkarasi mandapam is a newly built hall situated opposite to the marriage halls and bears the name of saindy queen, Mangayarkarasi who contributed to Saivism and Tamil language. To the south of Mangayarkarasi mandapam lies the Servaikarar Mandapam, a hall built by Marudu brothers in 1795.
  13. The Nagara mandapam (Hall of beating drums) lies opposite to Sundareswarar shrine was built by Achaya Rayar, the minister of Rani Mangammal in 1635. The Kolu Mandapam is a hall for displaying dolls during the Navarathri festival celebrated during Septemberâ€“October. This hall is situated in the second corridor of the Meenakshi shrine at the western side.


Udaipur City Palace

History of Udaipur City Palace

  1. It was built over a period of nearly 400 years, with contribution from serval rulers of the Mewar dynasty.
  2. It's construction began in 1553, started by Maharana Udai Singh II of the Sisodia Rajput Family as he shifted the capital from erstwhile Chittor to the new found city of Udaipur.
  3. the Palace is Located on the east bank of Lake Pichola and serval palace built within its complex.
  4. the City Palace in Udaipur was built in a flamboyant style and is considered the largest of its type in the state of Rajasthan.
  5. It was built atop a hill, in a fusion of the Rajasthan & Mughal architectural styles, providing a panaromic view of the city and its surrounding.
  6. Overlooking Lake Pichola, serval historic monuments like the Lake Palace, Jag Mandir, Jagdish Temple, Monsoon Palace & Neemach Mata Temple, are all in the vicinity of the palace complex.
  7. Nestled within the Aravali mountain range, these landmarks are associated in popular culture with the filming of the 1983 James Bond movie Octopussy.

Tso Moriri

History of Tosi Moriri

  1. the Lake is at an altitude 4,522 m (14,836 ft). It is the largest of the high altitude lakes entirely within India & entirely within Ladakh in this Trans-Himalayan biographic region.
  2. It is about 16 miles (26 km) north to south in length & two to three miles (3 to 5 km) wide.
  3. the Lake has no outlet at present & the water is brackish though not very perceptible to taste.
  4. the Lake is fed by springs & snow-melt from neighboring mountains.
  5. Most Water enters the Lake in two major streams systems, one entering the Lake from the north, the other from the southwest.
  6. Both stream systems include extensive marshes where they enter the lake. It formerly had an outlet to the south, but this has become blocked & the lake has become a endorheic lake.
  7. the Lake is Oligotrophic in nature, and its waters are alkaline.
  8. Accessibility to the lake is largely limited to summer season, though Karzok on the northwest shore & the military facilities on the eastern shores have year-round habitation.


Shaniwar Wada

History of Shaniwar Wada

  1. Shaniwarwada is a historical fortification in the city of Pune in Maharashtra, India.
  2. Built in 1732, it was the seat of the Peshwas of the Maratha Empire until 1818, when the Peshwas lost control to the British East India Company after the Third Anglo-Maratha War.
  3. Following the rise of the Maratha Empire, the palace become the center of Indian politics in the 18th century.
  4. the Fort was itself was largely destroyed in 1828 by unexplained fire, but the surviving strcutres are now maintained as a tourist site.
  5. It was supposed to be made entirely of stone but after the completion of the base floor or the first story, the people of Sata (the national capital) complained to the Shahu (King) saying that a stone monument can be sancitioned & built only by the Shahu (King) himself & not the Peshwas.
  6. Following this, an Official letter was written to the Peshwas stating that the remaining building had to be made of bricks & not stone.
  7. the Wada was then completed & upon being attacked by the British Artillery 90 years later, all the top six stories collapsed leaving only the stone base, which was immune to the British Artillery. Hence only the stone base of the Shaniwar Wada remains & can be seen even today in the older parts of Pune.
  8. by 1758, at least a thousand of people lived in the fort.
  9. in 1773, Narayanrao, who was the fifth & ruling Peshwa then, was murdered by guards on orders of his uncle Raghunathrao & aunt Anandibai.
  10. a Popular rumour says that Narayanrao's ghost still calls for help on full moon nights.
  11. Various people, working around the area, have allegedly reported such cries. Sound of Kaka mala wachwa (Uncle save me) by Narayanrao Peshawe after his death.
  12. in June 1818, Peshwa Bajirao II, abdicted his Gaddi (Throne) to Sir John Malcolm of the British East India Company & went into political exile at Bithoor, near Kanpur in Present-Day Uttar Pradesh, India.
  13. Peshwa Baji Rao I, Prime Minister to Chattrapati Shahu, laid the ceremonial foundation of his own residence on Saturday, January 10, 1730.
  14. It was named Shaniwarwada from the Marathi words Shaniwar (Saturday) and Wada (a general term for any residence complex).
  15. Teak was imported from the jungles of Junnar, stone was brought from the nearby quarries of Chinchwad, and Lime (mineral) was brought from the lime-belts of Jejuri. Shaniwarwada was completed in 1732, at a total cost of Rs. 16,110, a very large sum at the time.
  16. The opening ceremony was performed according to Hindu religious customs, on January 22, 1732, another Saturday chosen for being a particularly auspicious day.
  17. Later the Peshwas made several additions, including the fortification walls, with bastions and gates; court halls and other buildings; fountains and reservoirs.
  18. Currently, the perimeter fortification wall has five gateways and nine bastion towers, enclosing a garden complex with the foundations of the original buildings. It is situated near the Mula-Mutha River, in Kasba Peth.


Golkonda Fort

History of Golkonda Fort

  1. Golkonda is also known as Golconda, Gol Konda or Golla Konda (Shepherds Hill) is a citadel & fort in Southern India and was the Capital of the medieval Sultanate of the Qutb Shahi Dynasty (c. 1518 - 1687), is situated 11 km (6.8 mi) west of Hyderabad.
  2. It is also a tehsil of Hyderabad district, Telagana, Indai. The Region is known for the mines that have produced some of the World's most famous gems, including the Koh-i-Noor, the Hope Diamond, Nassak Diamond & the Noor-ul-Ain.
  3. Golkonda was originally known as Mankal. Golkonda Fort was built by the Kakatiya Dynasty as part of their western defenses along the lines of the Kondapalli Fort.
  4. The city & fortress were built on a granite hill that is 120 meters (390 ft) high, surrounded by massive battlements.
  5. the Forst was rebuilt & strenghtend by Rani Rudrama Devi & her successor Prataparudra.
  6. Later, the fort came under the control of the Musunuri Nayaks, who defeated the Tughlaqi army occupying Warangal. It was ceded by the Musunuri Kapaya Naidu to the Bahmani Sultanate as part of a treaty in 1364.
  7. Under the Bahmani Sultanate, Golkonda slowly rose to prominence. Sultan Quli Qutb-ul-Mulk (r. 1487â€“1543), sent as a governor of Telangana, established it as the seat of his government around 1501.
  8. Bahmani rule gradually weakened during this period, and Sultan Quli formally became independent in 1538, establishing the Qutb Shahi dynasty based in Golkonda.
  9. Over a period of 62 years, the mud fort was expanded by the first three Qutb Shahi sultans into the present structure, a massive fortification of granite extending around 5 km (3.1 mi) in circumference.
  10. It remained the capital of the Qutb Shahi dynasty until 1590 when the capital was shifted to Hyderabad. The Qutb Shahis expanded the fort, whose 7 km (4.3 mi) outer wall enclosed the city.
  11. The fort finally fell into ruin in 1687, after an eight year long siege leading to its fall at the hands of the Mughal emperor Aurangzeb.
  12. The Golkonda Fort used to have a vault where once the famous Koh-i-Noor and Hope diamonds were stored along with other diamonds.
